About

I'm a geospatial data scientist and development economist with over eight years of experience translating complex spatial, survey, and satellite data into insights that move policy and investment. My work spans gender equity, global health, digital infrastructure, and economic development — almost always across the Global South, and always with the question of who gets counted and who gets left out.

Most recently I'm at Surgo Health, continuing to bridge the gap between granular data and real-world decision-making. Before that, I spent four years at Fraym as Senior Economic Geographer, leading $15M+ engagements for the Gates Foundation, Johnson & Johnson Global Public Health, USAID, World Bank, and private investors — building hyperlocal models that put geospatial intelligence in the hands of practitioners who need it most across sub-Saharan Africa, MENA, and Asia.

Prior to Fraym, I was a Research Associate at Innovations for Poverty Action in Dhaka, leading field impact evaluation on women's digital finance in Bangladesh — managing 100+ enumerators, designing 15+ CAPI surveys, and working alongside World Bank researchers to understand what actually changes when women gain access to mobile money.

My research interests sit at the intersection of gender norms, health behaviour, digital access, and the methodological frontiers of survey and spatial science. I believe the best analysis is the kind that can be understood, acted on, and held accountable.
